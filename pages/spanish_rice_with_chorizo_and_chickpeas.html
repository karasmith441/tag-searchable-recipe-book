Baked Spanish Rice with Andouille and Chickpeas

#over an hour #rice #sausage #spanish

Equipment

  • Dutch Oven
  • Skillet
  • Oven

Ingredients

  • 2 Lbs Andouille
  • 6 Cloves of Garlic
  • 1 Medium Yellow Onion
  • 1 Tbsp Tomato Paste
  • 1 Tsp Paprika
  • A Pinch of Cinnamon
  • A Large Pinch Saffron Threads
  • 3 Cup Chicken or Vegetable Stock
  • 1/4 Cup Dry Sherry
  • One 15-oz. Can Chickpeas
  • Salt & Pepper
  • 1 1/2 Cups Short Grain Rice
  • Finely Chopped Chives (Garnish)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400 F and put the Chickpeas in a colander or strainer to drain.
  2. Slice the Andouille into 3/4-inch slices and start cooking in a Dutch oven on the stove.
  3. Meanwhile, char a very roughly chopped Onion and 6 Whole Cloves of Garlic in a dry skillet for a few minutes until browned.
  4. Squeeze the garlic out of its skin and put it and the charred Garlic in a food processor, then pulse until the minced alliums are about rice sized.
  5. Once the Andouille is done, remove it from the Dutch oven and add in the alliums. Cook until jammy.
  6. Add 1 Tbsp Tomato Paste and cook for 5 more minutes.
  7. Add 1/4 Cup Dry Sherry, bring to a boil for a few minutes, then add 3 Cup Chicken or Vegetable Stock, 1 Tsp Paprika, A Pinch of Cinnamon, A Large Pinch Saffron Threads , Chickpeas (drained), and the Andouille to the Dutch oven.
  8. Salt to taste, then add 1 1/2 Cups Short Grain Rice and bring back to a boil.
  9. While it is boiling, move the Dutch oven from the stove the oven and bake, uncovered, until the rice is al dente and the liquid is absorbed, 20–25 minutes.
  10. Remove from the oven, cover, and let stand until the rice is just tender, about 10 minutes. Garnish with Chives.
