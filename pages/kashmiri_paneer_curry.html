Kashmiri Paneer Curry

#indian #one pot #vegetarian

Batch and Time

makes 4 servings
takes an hour

Equipment

  • Cutting Board
  • Dutch Oven

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs Paneer
  • 1 Zucchini
  • 1.25 Cups water
  • 1.25 Cups Milk
  • 2 tbsp Cornstarch
  • Some oil (for the pan)
  • 6 Black Cardamom Pods
  • 4 Green Cardamom Pods
  • 4 whole Cloves
  • 1 tsp Cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp Fennel Seeds
  • 2-3 Dried Bay Leaves
  • 1 tbsp Ground Ginger
  • 1 tbsp Ground Turmeric
  • 1 tbsp Yellow Curry Powder
  • 2 tsp Garlic Powder
  • 1/4 tsp Asafoetida powder
  • A dash of Kashmiri Chili Powder

Serving

  • Rice or Naan

Instructions

  1. Chop 2 lbs Paneer into 1/2 inch cubes and a zucchini into 1/4 inch cubes; then fry the paneer in a very shallow layer of oil.
  2. Remove paneer from the pan, and add zucchini, 6 Black Cardamom Pods, 4 Green Cardamom Pods, 4 whole Cloves, 1 tsp Cumin seeds, and 1 tsp Fennel Seeds and toast until fragrant.
  3. Remove the zucchini and add 1/4 tsp Asafoetida powder with more oil, make sure the Asafoetida Powder is wholly in the oil and is frying (otherwise it will smell and taste bad).
  4. Once the Asafoetida Powder loses its raw smell, add 1 tbsp Ground Ginger, 1 tbsp Ground Turmeric, 1 tbsp Yellow Curry Powder, 2 tsp Garlic Powder, and A dash of Kashmiri Chili Powder.
  5. Once everything is fragrant, add 1.25 Cups water and bring to a boil. Let simmer for a couple minutes, then add 1.25 Cups Milk and 2-3 Dried Bay Leaves.
  6. Reduce until the sauce reaches your desired thickness. Use cornstarch to thicken if necessary. (Make sure to make a cornstarch slurry first, it will clump fast in the sauce)
  7. Add back the Paneer and Zucchini and let them cook down for a few minutes so that they absorb the sauce.
  8. Serve with Rice and/or Naan
