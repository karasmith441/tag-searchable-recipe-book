Coconut Curry

#chicken #curry #indian

Batch and Time

Makes 3 Servings (4 Servings with Rice)
Takes ~40 Minutes

Equipment

  • Dutch Oven

Ingredients

Curry

  • 1 Medium Onion
  • 3 Cloves of Garlic
  • 1 Zucchini
  • 2 Star Anise Pods/star anise
  • 1/2 Inch Ginger
  • 2 Tbsp Yellow Curry Powder
  • 1 Tbsp Red Curry Powder
  • 1 Tbsp Paprika
  • 1 Tbsp Tomato Paste
  • 2 Tsp Cumin
  • 2 Tsp Turmeric
  • 1 Tsp White Pepper
  • Salt & Pepper

Instructions

  1. Mince an Onion and 3 Cloves of Garlic, cut a Zucchini into 1/4 inch slices, and season the Chicken with the chicken spices.
  2. In an oiled Dutch Oven over high heat, sear the chicken until almost cooked through, about 10 minutes, flipping as necessary.
  3. Remove the chicken, reduce the heat to medium, and add the Onion, garlic, and Zucchini to the Dutch oven.
  4. Cook until the Onion just begins to soften, about 7 minutes. Then add the spices (1/2 Inch Ginger, 1 Tbsp Red Curry Powder, 2 Tbsp Yellow Curry Powder, 1 Tbsp Paprika, 2 Tsp Cumin, 2 Tsp Turmeric, 1 Tsp White Pepper) and Tomato Paste.
  5. Once the spices are toasted, add the Can of Coconut Milk and 2 Star Anise Pods/star anise. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for 5-10 minutes depending on how thick you want the curry.
  6. Cut up the Chicken into bite sized pieces and re-add to the curry base. Simmer until the Chicken is finished cooking, about 5 minutes.

Notes

1 Tsp Galangal really compliments the coconut, but it's a little tough to find so it's not in the main recipe.
