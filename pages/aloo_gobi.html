Aloo Gobi

#curry #dairy free #indian #vegan #vegetarian

Equipment

  • Cutting Board
  • Pan
  • Dutch Oven

Ingredients

  • Several Medium Potatoes
  • One Head of Cauliflower
  • 1 Onion
  • 4 Cloves of Garlic
  • 1/3 Cup Cashews
  • 4-5 Small Tomatoes
  • 1 Green Chili
  • 2 Black Cardamom Pods
  • 2 Tsp Cumin Seeds
  • 2 Tsp Coriander Powder
  • 2 Tsp Ginger
  • 1 1/2 Tsp Garam Masala
  • 1 Tsp Amchur Powder
  • 1/2 Tsp Kashmiri Chili Powder
  • 1/2 Tsp Ancho Chili Powder
  • 1/4 Tsp Turmeric
  • Some Neutral Oil
  • Salt

Instructions

  1. Soak 1/3 Cup Cashews in just enough water to cover them while prepping other ingredients. Chop Several Medium Potatoes into bite sized chunks, and break One Head of Cauliflower into similarly comfortably sized pieces.
  2. Roughly chop the Onion, then char the Onion and 4 Cloves of Garlic in a dry (no oil) Dutch oven or Skillet.
  3. Using a blender (immersion or otherwise), puree the Cashews until mostly smooth. Add tomatoes one at a time, blending until mostly smooth after each. Then add the charred Onion and Garlic a couple pieces at a time, once again, blending until mostly smooth after each addition.
  4. Heat oil in a skillet and a Dutch oven over medium heat until shimmering.
  5. In the skillet, lightly fry the potatoes until just browned, then add the Cauliflower, partially cover, and reduce to low heat.
  6. In the Dutch oven, fry 2 Tsp Cumin Seeds , and 2 Black Cardamom Pods . (If you have ground cumin wait to add it with the other spices.)
  7. While those are frying, chop up the Green Chili.
  8. Add the cashew/tomato sauce, Green Chili, 2 Tsp Coriander Powder , 2 Tsp Ginger, 1 1/2 Tsp Garam Masala, 1 Tsp Amchur Powder, 1/2 Tsp Kashmiri Chili Powder , 1/2 Tsp Ancho Chili Powder , and 1/4 Tsp Turmeric (if you didn't add the cumin earlier, add it now). Cook until the curry loses the raw tomato taste.
  9. Add the Potatoes and Cauliflower to the Dutch oven and simmer until they're done. If it starts to thicken more than you would like, just add some vegetable stock or just water. Once the veggies are done, salt to taste and adjust any other spices as necessary.
