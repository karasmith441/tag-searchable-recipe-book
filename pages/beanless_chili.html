Beanless Chili

#mexican #american #beef #dairy free #adjustable spice level #over an hour

Batch and Time

makes 8 servings
takes 4-5 hours

Equipment

  • Small Bowl
  • Pan
  • Cutting Board
  • Knives
  • Slow Cooker

Ingredients

  • 3-4 lbs Ground Beef
  • 4-5 Large Carrots
  • 1 Large Onion
  • One head Garlic
  • 1 Poblano Chili
  • 3-4 Chipotles in Adobo
  • 2 Cans Fire Roasted Tomatos
  • 1-2 Cans Tomato Sauce
  • 1/4 Cup Paprika
  • 1/4 Cup Cumin
  • 1 tsp Cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp Cloves
  • 1/2 tsp Nutmeg
  • A couple sprigs fresh Oregano and Thyme, Chopped.
  • Salt and Pepper

optional

  • 2-3 spoonfuls Adobo Sauce
  • 1 Bag Frozen Corn

Instructions

  1. Make the spice blend: In a small bowl, combine 1/4 Cup of Paprika, 1/4 Cup of Cumin, 1 tsp of Cinnamon, 1/2 tsp of Cloves, and 1/2 tsp of Nutmeg
  2. Dice 4-5 Large Carrots, 1 Large Onion, One head of Garlic, 1 Poblano Chili, and 3-4 Chipotles in Adobo to the size of your liking. I recommend that the carrots, onion, and poblano should be roughly bean sized and the rest smaller. Put them all in the slow cooker.
  3. Brown 3-4 lbs of Ground Beef in a ripping hot pan.
  4. Add meat, spice blend, sprigs of oregano and thyme, 2 Cans of Fire Roasted Tomatos, and 1-2 Cans of Tomato Sauce to the slow cooker. [Optional] If you want a little extra spice, a few spoonfuls of adobo sauce here are a welcome addition.
  5. Cover and cook on high for 3-4 hours, until carrots are almost cooked through.
  6. Uncover and reduce to low heat. Adjust spices and add salt and pepper to taste. Continue to cook until carrots are as cooked as you want them to be. [Optional] Add corn here.

Notes

Sour Cream and Cheddar are a very welcome addition and a squeeze of lime juice really brightens the meal.
This recipe also works well with meats other than beef, just watch out for the meat drying out.
